CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  • Check for responsiveness.
  • Call emergency services (112/108).
  • Begin 30 chest compressions at 100–120 bpm.
  • Give 2 rescue breaths.
  • Repeat until help arrives.

Choking

  • Ask: "Are you choking?"
  • Encourage coughing if mild.
  • If severe: Give 5 back blows + 5 abdominal thrusts.
  • Repeat until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious.

Burns

  • Cool the burn under running water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Do not apply ice or creams.
  • Cover with clean cloth or cling film.
  • Seek medical help if severe.

Head Injury

  • Keep the person still and lying down.
  • Stop any bleeding with gentle pressure.
  • Do not move the neck if spinal injury is suspected.
  • Call emergency services if unconscious or confused.

Bleeding

  • Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Keep the injured part elevated if possible.
  • Do not remove the cloth if soaked—add more layers.
  • Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop.

Snake Bite

  • Keep the person calm and still.
  • Immobilize the affected limb and keep it below heart level.
  • Do not cut or suck the bite.
  • Call emergency services and take the person to hospital immediately.

Fracture

  • Immobilize the injured area with a splint or sling.
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling.
  • Do not try to push the bone back.
  • Seek medical attention immediately.

Nosebleed

  • Lean the person forward slightly.
  • Pinch the soft part of the nose for 10 minutes.
  • Do not tilt the head backward.
  • If bleeding continues after 20 minutes, seek medical help.

Heatstroke

  • Move the person to a cool, shaded area.
  • Cool them with damp cloths or a fan.
  • Give small sips of water if conscious.
  • Call emergency services if unconscious or confused.

Fainting

  • Lay the person flat on their back and raise legs.
  • Loosen tight clothing.
  • Ensure fresh air and avoid crowding.
  • If unconscious for more than 2 minutes, seek help.

Poisoning

  • Do not induce vomiting unless told by a medical professional.
  • Identify the poison if possible.
  • Call emergency services or a poison helpline.
  • Monitor breathing and consciousness.

Emergency Numbers (India)

  • 🚨 General Emergency: 112
  • 🚑 Ambulance: 108
  • 🚒 Fire: 101
